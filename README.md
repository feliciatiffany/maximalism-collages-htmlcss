# 🎨 Maximalism Collages

> An interactive collage website exploring the question: "How maximalist are you?"

## 🌟 Highlights

- HTML and CSS-based collage with layered images and text
- Explores maximalism as a cultural movement and aesthetic lifestyle
- Multiple collage layouts (collage1, collage2, collage3)
- Vintage Memphis-inspired design elements
- Responsive design with bold, eclectic styling

## 📖 Overview

Maximalism is a cultural movement and an aesthetic lifestyle characterized by abundance and excess. Their saying, "more is more," positions it as a reaction against minimalism. From the Memphis era to today, maximalism has remained a recurring trend, even if its forms keep changing. Social media now sits at the center of shaping maximalist tastes, and influencers promoting the look are everywhere.

I find the trend fascinating; it even tempts me to buy objects whose designs are intentionally over the top. On one hand, I'm not strictly a maximalist but maybe I would consider myself one? That question inspired this collage website.

The idea behind "How maximalist are you?" isn't about precise measurement; it's a way to notice how widespread maximalism has become. Sometimes we just want a few eclectic pieces, and other times we want to surround ourselves with everything at once. Every person's choices are different and sometimes we can just be in between.

Open the project in a web browser:

1. Open `index.html` to start from the main collage
2. Navigate to `collage2.html` and `collage3.html` for additional variations

```

```

This is a static HTML/CSS website. No installation required.

Requirements:
- Any modern web browser (Chrome, Firefox, Safari, Edge)

The project embraces the maximalist aesthetic with:

- Eclectic typography and imagery
- Memphis-era inspired geometric shapes
- Intentional abundance and excess in design
